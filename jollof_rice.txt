Jollof Rice
Duration (minutes): 55
energy(kWh): 1.45

The legendary West African one-pot rice dish cooked in a rich tomato and pepper sauce with bold spices and a coveted smoky bottom crust. One serving (about 300g) contains approximately 500 calories.
Ingredients

    2 cups long grain parboiled rice
    4 pieces tomatoes
    2 pieces red bell pepper (tatashe)
    2 pieces scotch bonnet pepper
    2 pieces onion
    2 tablespoons tomato paste
    4 tablespoons vegetable oil
    2.5 cups chicken stock
    1 teaspoons curry powder
    1 teaspoons thyme
    2 pieces bay leaves
    2 pieces stock cube
    1 teaspoons salt
    1 tablespoons butter

Steps
Blend the pepper base: Blend 4 pieces tomatoes, 2 pieces red bell pepper (tatashe), 2 pieces scotch bonnet pepper, and 1 of the 2 pieces onion together until smooth. Set this pepper blend aside.
Fry the onion and tomato paste: Heat 4 tablespoons vegetable oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Slice the second 2 pieces onion and fry for 5 minutes . Add 2 tablespoons tomato paste and stir-fry for 3 minutes until it darkens slightly.
Cook down the pepper blend: Pour the blended pepper mix into the pot. Stir well, increase to medium-high heat, and fry uncovered for 20–25 minutes , stirring occasionally, until the raw taste is gone and oil floats on top.
Season the sauce: Add 1 teaspoons curry powder, 1 teaspoons thyme, 2 pieces bay leaves, 2 pieces stock cube, and 1 teaspoons salt into the cooked pepper base. Stir well and cook for another 2 minutes .
Add the rice and stock: Wash and drain 2 cups long grain parboiled rice, then add it directly into the seasoned pepper sauce. Pour in 2.5 cups chicken stock — the liquid should just about cover the rice. Stir once to combine.
Cook the jollof: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to the lowest setting. Cover tightly with foil first, then the pot lid (this traps all steam). Cook for 25–30 minutes without lifting the lid.
Check and stir gently: After 5 minutes , open the pot and check if the rice is cooked and liquid absorbed. If there is still liquid, cover again for another 5–10 minutes.
Add butter and rest: Once cooked, add 1 tablespoons butter on top, remove the bay leaves, and gently fold through. Turn off the heat, cover, and let rest for 5 minutes — this makes the rice fluffy and glossy.
