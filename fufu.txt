 Title Fufu

A classic West African staple — smooth, stretchy, and deeply satisfying. One serving (about 200g) contains approximately 320 calories.

ingredients

    2.2 pounds cassava (peeled and washed)
    2 pieces unripe plantain (peeled)
    3 cups water
    1 teaspoons salt

Steps
Prepare the cassava: Start with 2.2 pounds cassava (peeled and washed). Cut them into small chunks and rinse them thoroughly under cold water to remove any dirt or starch residue.
Prepare the plantain: Take 2 pieces unripe plantain (peeled) and cut them into chunks. Adding unripe plantain gives the fufu a firmer, stretchier texture — don't skip this step!
Boil together: Place the cassava and plantain chunks into a large pot. Pour in 3 cups water — enough to fully cover them. Bring to a boil over high heat and cook until both are very soft and tender.
Drain the water: Once fully cooked and soft, carefully drain all the water from the pot. Set the boiled cassava and plantain aside briefly.
Season and pound: Transfer the drained cassava and plantain into a mortar (or heavy-duty bowl). Add 1 teaspoons salt and begin pounding with a pestle. Pound firmly and consistently, folding the mixture as you go.
Pound to a smooth dough: Continue pounding and folding until the fufu becomes a completely smooth, stretchy, lump-free dough. This takes effort — keep going until there are no fibrous bits left.
Shape and serve: Wet your hands with a little water, then roll the fufu into round balls or shape as desired. Serve immediately while warm alongside your favourite soup or stew.
