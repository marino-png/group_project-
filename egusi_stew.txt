Egusi Stew

A rich, hearty West African stew made with ground melon seeds, leafy greens, and bold spices. One serving (about 250g) contains approximately 420 calories.

Ingredients

    2 cups ground egusi (melon seeds)
    4 tablespoons palm oil
    1 pieces onion (blended)
    3 pieces tomatoes (blended)
    2 pieces scotch bonnet pepper (blended)
    14.3 ounces beef or stockfish (cooked)
    2 cups spinach or ugu leaves (washed and chopped)
    2 pieces stock cube
    1 teaspoons salt
    2 tablespoons crayfish (ground)
    1 cups water

Steps
Mix the egusi paste: Pour 2 cups ground egusi (melon seeds) into a bowl. Add a little water and mix into a thick paste. Set aside — this will be fried directly in the oil later.
Heat the palm oil: In a large pot, heat 4 tablespoons palm oil over medium heat for about 2 minutes . Do not bleach (over-heat) the oil — just warm it until it melts and loosens.
Fry the onion and pepper blend: Add the blended 1 pieces onion (blended), 3 pieces tomatoes (blended), and 2 pieces scotch bonnet pepper (blended) into the hot oil. Stir well and fry on medium heat until the raw smell disappears and the oil begins to float on top.
Fry the egusi paste: Add the egusi paste into the pot in small lumps. Do NOT stir immediately — let it fry undisturbed for 3–4 minutes , then gently stir to break into chunks.
Add meat and seasoning: Stir in the cooked 14.3 ounces beef or stockfish (cooked), 2 tablespoons crayfish (ground), 2 pieces stock cube, and 1 teaspoons salt. Mix everything well so all the flavours combine.
Simmer with water: Pour in 1 cups water to loosen the stew. Stir, cover the pot, and simmer on low heat for 10 minutes until the egusi is fully cooked through.
Add the greens and finish: Stir in the chopped 2 cups spinach or ugu leaves (washed and chopped). Cook uncovered for a final 3–5 minutes until the leaves are wilted but still vibrant. Taste and adjust salt if needed.
Serve: Serve hot with fufu, pounded yam, eba, or rice. Enjoy!
